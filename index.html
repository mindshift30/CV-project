How AI Protects Forests

A multi-layered system of machine learning models continuously watches over millions of hectares of forest from space.

Computer Vision

Satellite Images & Object Detection

Multispectral satellite feeds are processed by convolutional neural networks that detect objects — roads, vehicles, sawmill equipment — that signal encroachment, even beneath partial cloud cover.

Change Detection

Forest Monitoring & Change Detection

AI compares satellite snapshots taken weeks or months apart, pinpointing pixel-level changes in canopy cover. Alerts fire within hours of detecting statistically significant loss.

Anomaly Detection

Illegal Logging Detection

Object detection models draw bounding boxes around clearings, skid trails, and log piles. Coordinates are cross-referenced with protected zone boundaries and reported to enforcement agencies in real time.

Thermal Analysis

Fire Detection & Heat Signatures

Infrared and SWIR satellite bands feed thermal anomaly models that identify active fires and predict spread direction. Early warnings reach ground teams in under 15 minutes.

Biodiversity

Environmental Analysis & Biodiversity Tracking

AI models estimate biomass, carbon stock, and species-habitat suitability from spectral signatures, helping conservation teams prioritize which areas need the most urgent protection.
